THE 1950s: FORMULA 1 BEGINS

1950

First World Championship Season

The Formula 1 World Championship officially began with the 1950 British Grand Prix at Silverstone. Giuseppe Farina won the race and went on to become the first World Champion driving for Alfa Romeo.

1951

Juan Manuel Fangio's First Title

Argentine legend Juan Manuel Fangio won his first of five World Championships. His dominance in the 1950s earned him the nickname "El Maestro" and established him as one of the greatest drivers in history.

1955

Mercedes Dominance

Mercedes-Benz entered F1 with the revolutionary W196, featuring streamlined bodywork and fuel injection. Fangio won 4 of the 6 races he entered that season before Mercedes withdrew after the Le Mans disaster.

1958

Constructors' Championship Introduced

The Constructors' Championship was introduced, with Vanwall becoming the first winners. Mike Hawthorn became Britain's first World Champion, but tragically died in a road accident just months after winning the title.

THE 1960s: BRITISH DOMINANCE

1961

Ferrari's Dominant 156

Ferrari introduced the revolutionary 156 "Sharknose" with a 120° V6 engine. American Phil Hill won the championship, but the season was marred by teammate Wolfgang von Trips' fatal accident at Monza.

1962

Lotus 25 - Monocoque Chassis

Colin Chapman's Lotus team introduced the 25 with a revolutionary monocoque chassis, replacing traditional spaceframe designs. This innovation would become standard in F1 car design.

1966

3.0L Engine Formula

New regulations introduced 3.0L engines, replacing the 1.5L formula. Jack Brabham won the championship in his own car, the only driver to do so in F1 history.

1968

Aerodynamic Wings & Sponsorship

Lotus introduced aerodynamic wings and the first major sponsorship (Gold Leaf), changing F1's financial model forever. Tragically, Jim Clark died in a Formula 2 race, shocking the motorsport world.

LEGENDARY CHAMPIONS

JUAN MANUEL FANGIO

5 World Championships

The "Maestro" dominated F1's early years with his incredible car control and tactical brilliance. Won championships with four different teams (Alfa Romeo, Ferrari, Mercedes, Maserati).

Years: 1950-1958 24 Wins

JIM CLARK

2 World Championships

Considered by many as the most naturally talented driver in F1 history. Dominated the 1960s with Lotus before his tragic death in 1968. Won 25 of 72 races entered.

Years: 1960-1968 25 Wins

AYRTON SENNA

3 World Championships

The Brazilian icon combined raw speed with relentless determination. His rivalry with Alain Prost defined late 1980s F1. Died tragically at Imola in 1994.

Years: 1984-1994 41 Wins

MICHAEL SCHUMACHER

7 World Championships

Redefined F1 excellence with his work ethic and technical feedback. Dominated with Benetton and Ferrari, setting records that stood for over a decade.

Years: 1991-2012 91 Wins
