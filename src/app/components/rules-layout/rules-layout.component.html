TECHNICAL REGULATIONS

CHASSIS

  • Minimum weight: 798kg (including driver)
  • Maximum width: 2000mm
  • Front wing width: 2000mm (max), 3-5 elements
  • Rear wing width: 1050mm (max), 2 elements
  • Ground effect underbody with Venturi tunnels

2023 Chassis Technical Diagram

POWER UNIT

  • 1.6L V6 turbocharged hybrid
  • 15,000 rpm maximum
  • MGU-K: 120kW (161hp) output
  • MGU-H: Recovers energy from turbo
  • Fuel flow limit: 100kg/hour (max)
  • 3 power units per driver per season

Power Unit Components

AERODYNAMICS

  • Simplified front wing with outwash prevention
  • Simplified rear wing with no cascades
  • No bargeboards or complex turning vanes
  • Wheel wake control devices (wheel covers)
  • Ground effect underbody with standardized diffuser

Aerodynamic Flow Visualization

TYRES

  • 18-inch diameter (was 13-inch until 2021)
  • 5 compounds: C1-C5 (hard to soft) + intermediates + wets
  • Minimum starting pressures regulated
  • Heating blankets limited to 70°C (front) and 50°C (rear)
  • 13 sets of dry tires allocated per weekend

Pirelli Tire Compounds

SAFETY COMPONENTS

  • Halo: 3rd generation titanium structure
  • Front and rear impact structures
  • Side impact protection panels
  • Fuel cell with crash-resistant construction
  • 6-point seat belts with quick release

Safety Features Diagram

OTHER TECHNICAL

  • Standardized ECU (Electronic Control Unit)
  • Fuel: 10% sustainable ethanol blend
  • No traction control or ABS
  • Limited telemetry data transmission
  • Cost cap on certain development areas
All components must pass FIA crash tests

SPORTING REGULATIONS

RACE WEEKEND FORMAT

  • Friday: 2 practice sessions (FP1 & FP2)
  • Saturday: FP3 + Qualifying (Q1, Q2, Q3)
  • Sunday: Race (305km max distance, ~2hr time limit)
  • 6 Sprint weekends with different format (FP1, Qualifying, Sprint, Race)
  • Parc fermé rules after qualifying (limited setup changes)

Qualifying Elimination:

  • Q1: 18 min, bottom 5 eliminated
  • Q2: 15 min, bottom 5 eliminated
  • Q3: 12 min, determines top 10

RACE PROCEDURES

  • Formation lap: Determines starting order
  • Standing starts: From grid positions
  • Pit stops: Minimum 2.5 sec stationary (with tire change)
  • Safety Car: Neutralizes race, no overtaking
  • Virtual Safety Car: Maintain gaps, speed limited
  • Red Flag: Race stopped, may be restarted

Points System:

1st: 25
2nd: 18
3rd: 15
4th: 12
5th: 10
6th: 8
7th: 6
8th: 4
9th: 2
10th: 1

+1 point for fastest lap (if in top 10)

DRIVER REQUIREMENTS

  • Super License required (40 points from junior series)
  • Minimum age: 18 years
  • Mandatory FIA medical checks
  • Helmet designs must be approved
  • Driver weight minimum: 80kg (including seat)

Super License Points Example:

  • 1st in F2: 40 pts
  • 1st in F3: 30 pts
  • 1st in W Series: 15 pts
  • 1st in IndyCar: 40 pts

TEAM REQUIREMENTS

  • Maximum 2 cars per team
  • Minimum 2 race drivers per season
  • 1 reserve driver must be nominated
  • Limited testing (3 pre-season + 2 in-season days)
  • Wind tunnel and CFD restrictions

Team Personnel:

  • Maximum 60 team personnel at track
  • Only 4 mechanics allowed per car during pit stops
  • Team principal must be registered

FINANCIAL REGULATIONS

COST CAP

  • 2023 cap: $135 million (21-race season)
  • Applies to performance-related spending
  • Excludes: Driver salaries, top 3 personnel salaries, marketing, travel
  • Inflation adjustment mechanism
  • Audits conducted by FIA

Penalties for Breach:

  • Minor overspend: Financial penalty + reduction in testing
  • Major overspend: Points deduction, possible exclusion

OTHER FINANCIAL RULES

  • Prize money distribution based on championship position
  • Long-standing teams receive heritage payments
  • New teams must pay $200m anti-dilution fee
  • Limited sponsorship from tobacco companies
  • Restrictions on manufacturer payments to customer teams

Development Restrictions:

  • Wind tunnel time based on championship position
  • CFD simulation limits
  • Restricted number of upgrades per season

PENALTIES

ON-TRACK INFRACTIONS

  • False start: 5 or 10 sec stop/go
  • Speeding in pit lane: Fine or time penalty
  • Unsafe release: 5 or 10 sec stop/go
  • Causing a collision: 5-10 sec or grid drop
  • Ignoring blue flags: 5 sec or drive-through
  • Track limits violations: Lap times deleted

Penalty Points System:

  • Points stay on license for 12 months
  • 12 points = automatic race ban
  • Typical infraction: 1-3 points

TECHNICAL INFRACTIONS

  • Illegal car parts: Disqualification
  • Underweight car: Disqualification
  • Fuel flow violations: Disqualification
  • Minor technical breaches: Grid penalties
  • Power unit component changes: Grid drops

Power Unit Penalties:

  • ICE: 10 places
  • MGU-K: 5 places
  • Turbo: 5 places
  • Exceeding allocation: Back of grid

SAFETY REGULATIONS

CAR SAFETY

  • Halo: Mandatory titanium structure
  • Headrest: High-impact foam padding
  • Fuel cell: Crash-resistant construction
  • Extinguishers: Automatic and manual systems
  • Wheel tethers: Keep wheels attached in crashes

Crash Test Requirements:

  • Frontal impact at 15m/s
  • Rear impact at 11m/s
  • Side impact at 10m/s
  • Roll structure must withstand 60kN

DRIVER SAFETY

  • Helmet: FIA 8860-2018 standard
  • HANS device: Head and neck support
  • Race suit: Multi-layer fireproof material
  • Gloves & shoes: Fireproof materials
  • Medical car: Follows field for immediate response

Helmet Standards:

  • Withstands 800°C for 45 seconds
  • Impact absorption up to 300G
  • Visor penetration resistance

TRACK SAFETY

  • Run-off areas: Minimum gravel trap/Tarmac
  • Barriers: SAFER/Tecpro/Tire walls
  • Marshals: Trained personnel at key points
  • Medical center: Fully equipped on-site
  • Lighting: Flags and LED panels

Flag Meanings:

Yellow: Danger
Red: Session stopped
Green: Track clear
Blue: Faster car

PROCEDURAL SAFETY

  • Virtual Safety Car: Speed limited to prevent accidents
  • Safety Car: Neutralizes race for incident clearance
  • Red Flag: Stops session for major incidents
  • Medical checks: Mandatory after big impacts
  • Extraction practice: Regular drills for marshals

Safety Innovations:

  • Halo introduced in 2018
  • Headrest improvements after 2016
  • Stronger wheel tethers after 2020
