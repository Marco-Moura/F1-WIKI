YOUR FORMULA 1 CAREER STARTS HERE

Formula 1 teams employ over 1,000 people across various disciplines. While becoming an F1 driver is the most visible path, it's just one of many ways to join the pinnacle of motorsport. This guide covers all official pathways based on FIA regulations and team recruitment practices.

Important Note

All F1 personnel must pass FIA background checks and comply with the International Sporting Code. Some roles require specific licenses or certifications as outlined in the FIA regulations.

BECOMING AN F1 DRIVER

FIA SUPER LICENSE REQUIREMENTS

Minimum Age: 18 years old
Points Requirement: 40 points over 3 years from eligible series
Driving Test: Pass FIA driver ability test in current F1 car
Medical: Pass FIA medical examination
Ethics: Clean disciplinary record

License Cost:

€10,000 + €2,100 per point from previous season

BECOMING AN F1 ENGINEER

RACE ENGINEER

Education: Engineering degree (Masters preferred)
Experience: 5+ years in motorsport
Skills: Data analysis, communication, leadership
Pathway: Junior engineer → Performance engineer → Race engineer

Key Responsibilities:

  • - Driver communication during sessions
  • - Strategy decisions
  • - Car setup direction

DESIGN ENGINEER

Education: Mechanical/Aerospace engineering degree
Software: CAD (CATIA, NX), CFD, FEA
Experience: 3+ years in high-performance engineering
Pathway: CAD technician → Design engineer → Chief designer

Key Responsibilities:

  • - Component design
  • - Stress analysis
  • - Innovation within regulations

PERFORMANCE ENGINEER

Education: Engineering/physics degree
Software: MATLAB, Python, ATLAS
Experience: 3+ years in data analysis
Pathway: Data analyst → Performance engineer → Chief engineer

Key Responsibilities:

  • - Live data monitoring
  • - Simulation correlation
  • - Performance optimization

Getting Your First Role

Most engineers start in junior series (F2, F3, FE) or as interns in F1 teams. Key entry points are:

  • - Industrial placements during degree
  • - Graduate schemes (e.g., Mercedes AMG HPP)
  • - Junior engineering roles in smaller teams

BECOMING AN F1 MECHANIC

RACE MECHANIC

Education: Motorsport/Motor Vehicle qualification
Experience: 3+ years in professional motorsport
Skills: Precision work, speed, teamwork
Pathway: Workshop technician → Garage mechanic → Race mechanic

Key Responsibilities:

  • - Car assembly/disassembly
  • - Pit stop performance
  • - Track-side repairs

COMPOSITE TECHNICIAN

Education: Composites engineering qualification
Experience: 2+ years in composite fabrication
Skills: Layup techniques, autoclave operation
Pathway: Apprentice → Composite tech → Senior tech

Key Responsibilities:

  • - Carbon fiber component manufacture
  • - Repair work
  • - Quality control

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

For most roles, the minimum age is 18 due to travel and insurance requirements. Junior driver programs may accept younger candidates (typically 13+ for karting programs that feed into F1 academies).

Most F1 teams are based in the UK (70%), Italy (20%), or Switzerland (10%). Factory-based roles will require relocation to these areas. Travel roles (e.g., race team) typically live near the factory but travel 50% of the year.

Extremely competitive. Top teams receive 500+ applications per engineering role. Your best chance is to:

  • - Gain experience in junior formulas first
  • - Complete relevant internships
  • - Develop specialized skills (e.g., CFD, battery tech)
  • - Network within the industry

Salaries vary widely by role and experience:

  • - Graduate engineers: £25,000-£35,000
  • - Senior engineers: £50,000-£90,000
  • - Chief engineers: £100,000+
  • - Race mechanics: £30,000-£60,000
  • - Drivers: £150,000 to £50M+

Note: Salaries are typically lower than equivalent automotive industry roles due to high demand to work in F1.

For technical roles, a degree is typically required. However, for these positions you may not need one:

  • - Mechanics (apprenticeship path available)
  • - Pit crew (athletic background can substitute)
  • - Hospitality/logistics (experience valued over degrees)
  • - Media roles (portfolio often more important)

FIA accreditation is required for all personnel working at races. Your F1 team will typically apply for this on your behalf once hired. Requirements include:

  • - Clean criminal record
  • - Medical clearance
  • - Proof of employment with licensed team
  • - Completion of FIA ethics training
